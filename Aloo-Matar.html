Recipe

Restaurant style paneer tikka masala

Procedure with ingredients:
Take ½ cup chopped onions, 1 cup chopped tomatoes, 1 teaspoon chopped ginger (chopped) and 1 teaspoon chopped garlic in a grinder or blender jar. Without adding any water, grind or blend to a smooth paste.
Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a thick-bottomed pot or pressure cooker. Add ½ teaspoon cumin seeds and cook until they crackle.
You can use any neutral flavored oil. For a richer taste you can also use ghee (clarified butter).
Now add the ground onion-tomato paste to the oil in the pan. Keep the heat to a low and also take care as the mixture splutters.
Stir well and then add 1 tablespoon malai (the thick layer of cream floating on top of boiled cooled whole milk).
Tip: The curry masala mixture splutters, so cover the pot or cooker partly with a lid.
Once the mixture stops spluttering, then remove the lid and sauté.
Stir often so that the curry masala does not get browned or gets stuck to the base of the pot.
Sauté the ground paste till the oil releases from the sides and the masala paste thickens. You will see that the paste becomes glossy and comes together in a kind of lump.
Then add a generous pinch of asafoetida (hing), ½ teaspoon red chili powder and ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder. Stir and mix the spice powders with the rest of the sautéed masala paste.
Add 1 cup green peas and 2 large potatoes (peeled and diced) to the curry masala paste.
Stir and sauté for a minute.
If cooking in a pot, add 1.5 cups of water.
Season with salt as required.
Stir well and cover the pot with the lid.
Simmer till the potatoes and peas are cooked tender. If necessary, you can add some more water.
Lastly sprinkle in ½ teaspoon garam masala powder. Stir again.
Then add 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (cilantro).
Stir well and serve Aloo Matar hot from the pan, preferably with Indian breads like poori, roti, paratha, naan or serve with steamed rice or cumin rice.
